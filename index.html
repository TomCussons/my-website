Work

I led various large projects during my time at HSBC, using a variety of coding languages. Below shows a couple of highlights:

GCP Data Restructure

During HSBC's transition from SAS EFM to Feedzai, there was also a transition of data storage (from Teradata to GCP). Part of this move involved all transactional and behavioural data, this was initially deployed as one large dataset. Due to my concerns about data integrity and digestibility, I did a deep dive into the before and after costs.
This structure was sub-optimal, and I found that the costs to this were growing exponentially. I led a project for an entire new data structure, which led to faster and more cost-efficient cloud based data queries. This had an estimated saving of ~$1 Million per product per region per year.

Time Sheets - Entirely within VBA

The head of RCR analytics approached and asked me to build an internal time sheet for 100+ colleagues within the department. This needed to be: Uneditable after 2 weeks, private (everybody had their own account and password), allow multiple users to access and update at the same time, easy to use and feed into a managers dashboard for their own analytics.
I was approached on this due to my efficiency within Excel and VBA.
Within two weeks I managed to get the first version deployed, with all the key functionality achieved. This was entirely built within Excel and VBA.
Numerous iterations and version upgrades were implemented on.
I then trained and handed over the responsibility and maintenance to other colleagues as I moved on within my career.

Cross Product - Strategy Revamp

During my time within the Loans Acquisition team, I led a cross-department project to "Revamp" every products acquisition code.
When I initially joined the team, each product and brand had their own strategy codes. Large portions of these codes and rules were meant to be exactly the same cross product (i.e. the affordability calculations, bad bureau rules etc), however there were numerous misalignments causing unnecessary confusion and errors.
Due to my efficient and readable style of SAS, SQL & Macro coding, I redesigned the entirety of the HSBC loans code, making strong use of global and local macros. I then utilised the macros for M&S and finally First Direct. This then grew to be used and implemented for the other products within the department (Credit Cards & Current Accounts). Note: Brands were also split by channel
The end result of this project was having one universal source code that is now referenced by multiple strategies, meaning that any change requests only had one source to change rather than several. It also made analysts "Brand and Product agnostic", as multiple strategy rules and components were now fully aligned, allowing a analyst to switch between them with more ease.
